Activities Wild Atlantic

Hiking & Walking Trails

    Slieve League Cliffs (Donegal): One of the highest sea cliffs in Europe, offering jaw-dropping views.
    Connemara National Park (Galway): Explore mountains, bogs, and woodlands with several well-marked trails.
    The Burren (Clare): Walk through a unique limestone landscape full of wildlife and ancient archaeological sites.

Surfing

    Lahinch (Clare): Known as one of Ireland’s top surf spots, offering lessons and rental gear.
    Bundoran (Donegal): A favorite for surfers, with great waves year-round.
    Easkey (Sligo): A popular surf destination for both beginners and seasoned surfers.

Cycling

    There are numerous scenic cycling routes, including the Atlantic Way Cycle Route, where you can bike along quiet roads with dramatic coastal views.
    Great Western Greenway (Mayo): A 42 km long route that takes you through beautiful landscapes, from Westport to Achill Island.

Kayaking & Stand-Up Paddleboarding

    Killary Harbour (Galway): Ireland’s only fjord, perfect for kayaking.
    Inisheer Island (Aran Islands): Paddle through crystal-clear waters, surrounded by the rugged beauty of the islands.

Wildlife Watching

    Dingle Peninsula (Kerry): Look for seals, dolphins, and a wide range of seabirds.
    Killary Harbour (Galway): Watch for dolphins and explore its marine life by boat.
    Blasket Islands (Kerry): A haven for birdwatching, including puffins and other seabirds.
